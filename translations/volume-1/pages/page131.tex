The investigation of \textit{"singular series remainders"} of the form: $\lim_{n=\infty} \sum_{\nu=n+1}^{n+\varphi(n)} a_\nu$ required for this and completely feasible for the case $\lim \frac{a_{\nu+1}}{a_\nu} = 1$ teaches that the value changes in question depend not on the special formation law of the $a_\nu$, but solely on their behavior for $\lim \nu = \infty$: If $\lim \sum_{\nu=n+1}^{n+\varphi(n)} a_\nu = a$ (finite), then also $\lim \sum_{\nu=n+1}^{n+\varphi(n)} a'_\nu = a$, if $a'_\nu \cong a_\nu$; on the other hand $\lim \sum_{\nu=n+1}^{n+\varphi(n)} a'_\nu = 0$ or $= \infty$, if $a'_\nu \prec a_\nu$ or $\succ a_\nu$ respectively.
